Smashed Potatoes

Description

Strap into your seats as your taste buds are about to take off. Okay, maybe not that serious. However, these are some tasty, easy and heathly spuds that are perfect as a side dish to pretty much anything.

This recipe is perfect for those who are new to the kitchen and looking to have an easy go-to recipe when you grow tired of ordering fries all the time. Just make sure to have the below ingredients on hand and you have a one way ticket to tastetown.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Potatoes (Note: any size can work and you may just need to cut them differently)
  • 1 tbsp salt (for boiling)
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 3/4 tsp salt (for sprinkling)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • Old Bay Seasoning, optional garnish (but do it)
    •

    Steps

    1. Cook potatoes: Bring a pot of water to the boil, add 1 tbsp salt. Cook potatoes until soft AKA Fork Tender.
    2. Preheat oven to 390Â°F.
    3. Steam dry: Drain the potatoes and let them dry in the colander for 5 minutes.
    4. Smashing Time. Place on the tray then use a large fork, keeping them in one piece. Thin = crisper. Thicker = fluffier insides. (Both good!) More nubbly surface = better crunch!
    5. Steam dry again: Leave on the tray to steam dry for 5 minutes to aid in making them crispier.
    6. Party Time: Drizzle with butter, then olive oil. Sprinkle with salt and pepper as well as Old Bay if using.
    7. Bake: Bake for 45 minutes to 55 minutes or until deep golden and crispy. Do not flip.
    8. Serve hot, sprinkled with additional if desired.
